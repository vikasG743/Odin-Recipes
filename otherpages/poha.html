POHA Recipe

step by step recipe for yummy poha


  1. Rinse Poha: Wash 1.5 cups of thick/medium poha in a colander under running water for 15–20 seconds until soft but not mushy, then set aside to drain.
  2. Season Rice: Sprinkle salt, turmeric, and a pinch of sugar (optional) over the wet poha and toss gently.
  3. Fry Peanuts: Heat 1.5 tablespoons of oil in a pan, fry 2 tablespoons of peanuts until crunchy, then remove and set aside.
  4. Temper Spices: In the same oil, add 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds; once they splutter, add 1/2 teaspoon cumin, curry leaves, and green chillies.
  5. Sauté Aromatics: Add one finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent (add small diced potatoes now if desired and cook until tender).
  6. Combine Ingredients: Add the seasoned poha and fried peanuts to the pan, mixing gently with a light hand to avoid breaking the flakes.
  7. Steam: Cover the pan and let it steam on the lowest heat for 2–3 minutes to meld the flavours.
  8. Final Touch: Turn off the heat, squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top, and mix once more.
  9. Garnish & Serve: Top with fresh coriander leaves, nylon sev, or grated coconut.

Pro Tips for Success

  • The Squeeze Test: To ensure the poha is ready, press a flake between your thumb and forefinger; it should mash easily.
  • Avoid Dryness: If the poha feels too dry after mixing, sprinkle 1–2 tablespoons of water over it before steaming.
  • Regional Twist: For Indori Poha, add fennel seeds (saunf) to the tempering and serve with Jeeravan Masala.
